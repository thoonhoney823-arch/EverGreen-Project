Yangon Through The Lens

Visual Stories of Yangon's Green Spaces

Yangon is a city of remarkable natural beauty. From the shimmering waters of Kandawgyi Lake to the ancient trees of People's Park, the city's green spaces are treasures that deserve protection and expansion. This gallery showcases Yangon's existing natural beauty and imagines what a greener future could look like.

Downtown Yangon

🏙️

Yangon Skyline at Sunset

The iconic Yangon skyline with Shwedagon Pagoda glowing above the city, viewed from across the Kandawgyi Lake at sunset.

🏛️

Colonial Heritage Buildings

Downtown Yangon's colonial-era buildings, some of the best-preserved in Southeast Asia, lined along tree-shaded streets.

🌆

Busy City Streets

The energy and movement of downtown Yangon's streets — a vibrant city that needs more green spaces to balance its rapid growth.

Kandawgyi at Dawn

The calm waters of Kandawgyi Lake reflecting the early morning sky, with the Karaweik Palace floating gracefully on the water.

Lakeside Wildlife

Water birds and wildlife that make Kandawgyi Lake their home, demonstrating the biodiversity value of urban waterways.

Tree-Lined Walkways

The walking paths around Kandawgyi Lake, shaded by mature trees, offering residents a peaceful escape from the city.

Inya Lake

🏖️

Inya Lake Shoreline

The largest lake in Yangon, surrounded by greenery and providing essential cooling and flood absorption for the surrounding area.

🚣

Recreation on the Water

Boating and recreation on Inya Lake — green spaces provide both environmental benefits and opportunities for healthy leisure activities.

🌿

Riparian Vegetation

Trees and plants along Inya Lake's shoreline filter runoff, prevent erosion, and provide habitat for urban wildlife.

Category

Parks and Public Gardens

🏞️

People's Park

One of Yangon's largest green spaces, People's Park provides a vital refuge for residents seeking nature, exercise, and relaxation in the heart of the city.

🏛️

Maha Bandula Park

The historic Maha Bandula Park in downtown Yangon, surrounded by heritage buildings and shaded by ancient rain trees — a green oasis in the urban core.

🌺

Botanical Gardens

Flower gardens and botanical collections in Yangon's parks showcase the beauty and diversity of Myanmar's native plant species.

Tree-Lined Roads and Green Streets

🌳

University Avenue

Mature rain trees forming a green canopy over University Avenue, demonstrating the cooling and beautifying power of street trees.

🛣️

Shaded Boulevards

Where roads have retained their trees, the difference is dramatic — cooler air, cleaner streets, and a more pleasant environment for everyone.

🚶

Walkable Green Streets

Tree-lined sidewalks encourage walking and create a pleasant pedestrian experience — exactly what Yangon needs more of.

Biodiversity and Urban Nature

🦜

Urban Birdlife

Yangon's trees support diverse bird species, from colorful kingfishers to majestic kites, enriching the city's natural environment.

🦋

Butterflies and Pollinators

Butterflies, bees, and other pollinators depend on flowering trees and plants in Yangon's parks and gardens to survive.

🐢

Wetland Wildlife

Yangon's wetlands and waterways support turtles, fish, and aquatic plants — biodiversity that must be protected from pollution and development.

Future Smart Green Yangon Concepts

Green City Skyline

A vision of Yangon's future skyline — where green roofs, vertical gardens, and tree-lined streets create a city that lives in harmony with nature.

📡🌳
