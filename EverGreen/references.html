Our Sources

Trusted Sources and References

Accurate information is the foundation of any credible project. Throughout our research, we relied on data, reports, and publications from the world's most trusted environmental, health, and development organizations. Below are the sources we referenced when creating this website. We encourage visitors to explore these resources to learn more about the topics we discuss.

🌍 United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP)

UNEP is the leading global environmental authority that sets the global environmental agenda, promotes the environmental dimension of sustainable development, and serves as an authoritative advocate for the global environment. We referenced UNEP data on urban air quality, green infrastructure benefits, and sustainable city planning.

www.unep.org

🏥 World Health Organization (WHO)

WHO is the directing and coordinating authority for health within the United Nations system. We used WHO data on the health impacts of air pollution, the benefits of green spaces for physical and mental health, and recommended minimum green space per capita for urban areas (9 square meters per person).

www.who.int

🐾 World Wildlife Fund (WWF)

WWF is one of the world's largest conservation organizations, working to preserve biodiversity and reduce humanity's footprint on the natural environment. We referenced WWF research on biodiversity loss, habitat destruction, and the importance of wildlife corridors in urban areas.

www.wwf.org

🏦 World Bank

The World Bank provides financial and technical assistance to developing countries. Their urban development reports and environmental data for Myanmar provided important context about Yangon's growth challenges, infrastructure needs, and the economic case for green infrastructure investment.

www.worldbank.org

🏘️ United Nations Human Settlements Programme (UN-Habitat)

UN-Habitat is the United Nations agency for human settlements and sustainable urban development. Their reports on Yangon's urban planning challenges, green space distribution, and sustainable development strategies provided essential background for our project.

unhabitat.org

🛰️ NASA Climate

NASA's Climate division provides satellite data and scientific research on climate change, carbon cycles, and the role of forests in absorbing carbon dioxide. We referenced NASA data on the carbon absorption capacity of trees and the urban heat island effect.

climate.nasa.gov

🌍 National Geographic Environment

National Geographic's Environment section provides accessible, scientifically accurate articles on environmental issues, biodiversity, and conservation. Their educational resources helped us understand and communicate complex environmental concepts clearly.

www.nationalgeographic.com/environment

🎯 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals provide a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet. Our project aligns most closely with SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), SDG 13 (Climate Action), and SDG 15 (Life on Land).

www.un.org/sustainabledevelopment

🌿 Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)

The United States Environmental Protection Agency provides extensive research on air quality, the benefits of urban trees, and the health effects of pollution. We referenced EPA data on how trees remove air pollutants and reduce urban temperatures.

www.epa.gov

🦏 International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN)

IUCN is the world's largest and most diverse environmental network. Their Red List of Threatened Species and research on biodiversity conservation provided important information about species loss and the role of urban green spaces in supporting wildlife populations.

www.iucn.org

📝 A Note on Our Sources

As Year 7 students, we have done our best to use accurate and reliable information from trusted sources. However, some statistics and data points on this website may be approximate or represent general findings from the organizations listed above rather than Myanmar-specific data. We encourage anyone using this website for research to verify specific facts by visiting the original sources directly. This project was created for educational purposes as part of our Smart City unit at EDUSN Thingangyun Campus.
