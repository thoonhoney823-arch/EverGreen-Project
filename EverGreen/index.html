Introduction

How Can Trees Make Yangon a Smarter City?

Yangon is the largest city in Myanmar, home to over 5 million people. As the city grows rapidly, it faces serious environmental challenges — from air pollution and flooding to rising temperatures and loss of green spaces. Our project asks: How can planting more trees and increasing green spaces help transform Yangon into a smarter, healthier, and more sustainable city?

Trees are not just beautiful — they are powerful natural tools that clean our air, cool our neighborhoods, reduce flooding, support wildlife, and improve our health. When combined with smart city technology, urban trees become even more effective. This is the story of how a greener Yangon can be a smarter Yangon.

Awareness

Why Does This Matter?

🌍 The Challenge

Yangon is losing its green spaces at an alarming rate. Rapid urban development, road construction, and population growth are replacing trees with concrete. The city's tree canopy has been shrinking, and the consequences are already visible — hotter temperatures, worse air quality, more flooding, and declining wildlife.

🌱 The Opportunity

By strategically planting trees, creating parks, building green corridors, and integrating smart technology, Yangon can reverse this trend. Cities around the world have shown that investing in urban green infrastructure pays for itself many times over — in healthier people, cleaner environments, stronger communities, and more resilient cities.

Why Trees Matter

The Power of Urban Trees

Trees are nature's multi-tool. Here is how they help cities like Yangon.

💨

Trees → Cleaner Air

Trees absorb harmful pollutants like nitrogen dioxide, sulfur dioxide, and carbon monoxide. A single large tree can provide enough oxygen for four people in a day and filter up to 45 kg of air pollution each year.

🌡️

Trees → Cooler Temperature

Trees provide shade and release water vapor through transpiration, cooling the air around them. Urban areas with trees can be 2–8°C cooler than areas without trees, reducing the urban heat island effect.

🌊

Trees → Less Flooding

Tree roots absorb rainwater and reduce surface runoff. A single mature tree can absorb up to 450 liters of water per day, helping to prevent urban flooding during heavy monsoon rains in Yangon.

🦋

Trees → More Biodiversity

Trees provide food, shelter, and nesting sites for birds, insects, and other wildlife. Urban trees create corridors that connect fragmented habitats and support pollinators essential for food production.

❤️

Trees → Better Health

Living near trees reduces stress, lowers blood pressure, and improves mental health. Studies show that people in greener neighborhoods have lower rates of heart disease, asthma, and anxiety.

🤝

Trees → Better Communities

Green spaces bring people together. Parks and tree-lined streets encourage outdoor activity, community events, and social interaction, creating stronger and safer neighborhoods.
