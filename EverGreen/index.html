Urban Challenges

Challenges Facing
Yangon Today

Yangon is rapidly developing, but urban growth has reduced natural green spaces. This creates serious problems for our health, environment, and quality of life.

🏙️

Lack of Green Spaces

Expansion of buildings and roads has replaced trees and parks. Many neighborhoods have limited public parks and fewer trees than before.

Trees provide shade, cleaner air, and cooler neighborhoods for communities.
🏭

Air Pollution

Traffic congestion, vehicle emissions, and industrial activities severely degrade Yangon's air quality, posing serious health risks to citizens.

Trees naturally filter pollutants and produce oxygen, improving the air we breathe.
🌡️

Urban Heat

Concrete and asphalt absorb and trap heat. Without enough tree cover, Yangon's neighborhoods are becoming significantly hotter.

Tree canopies provide natural shade and cool the air through evapotranspiration.
🦜

Loss of Biodiversity

Fewer trees mean fewer birds, insects, and pollinators. Habitat loss is threatening Yangon's urban wildlife and ecosystems.

Planting native trees restores habitats and brings wildlife back to the city.
🌧️

Flooding

During the rainy season, rainwater runoff overwhelms drainage. Without natural absorption from trees and soil, flooding worsens.

Tree roots absorb water and green spaces reduce runoff, preventing floods.
Green Solutions

Building a Greener
Yangon

Trees and green spaces are the foundation of a smarter, healthier city. Here is how we can use nature and technology together to transform Yangon.

🌳

Tree Planting Programs

Organized campaigns to plant thousands of native trees across Yangon's streets, schools, and neighborhoods.

Cleaner air, more oxygen, cooler temperatures, better biodiversity
🏞️

Smart Urban Parks

Parks with solar-powered lighting, smart irrigation systems, digital information boards, and community spaces.

Efficient green spaces that educate and connect communities
🏫

Green School Campaigns

Students planting trees, maintaining gardens, promoting awareness, and participating in environmental projects.

Youth empowerment and hands-on environmental education
🌱

Vertical Gardens

Plant-covered walls and green facades that bring nature into dense urban areas where space is limited.

Urban greening without requiring ground-level space
🏡

Green Rooftops

Gardens on building rooftops that reduce temperatures, improve air quality, and increase biodiversity.

Temperature reduction, improved air quality, more habitat
📡

Smart Tree Monitoring

Sensors that monitor tree growth, soil moisture, and tree health — connecting sustainability with Smart City innovation.

Data-driven tree care and smarter urban forest management
